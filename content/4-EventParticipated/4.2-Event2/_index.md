## Reflection on "AWS Vietnam Community Day 2026"

**Time:** 09:00, May 23, 2026

### Event Purpose

* Update participants on the latest trends in Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence (AI).
* Learn how businesses apply AI and AWS in real-world scenarios.
* Connect with the technology community and learn from experts' experience.
* Help students orient their careers in Cloud and AI.

### Speakers

* **Anh Tinh** - Build Second Brain: Context is Everything.
* **Nguyen Tuan Thinh** - From Edge to Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation.
* **Pham Ng Hai Anh** - Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Q.
* **Team VIB** - 36 Hours with LotusHacks: Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality.
* **Dao Duc** - Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings.
* **Lam Cat Vy** - Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System.

### Key Highlights

#### Build Second Brain: Context is Everything

The speaker emphasized that in the AI era, data and context play an extremely important role. A powerful AI model does not necessarily produce good results if it lacks the right context.

Key lessons included:

* Context determines the quality of AI output.
* Personal knowledge should be organized so AI can provide more effective support.
* Building a "Second Brain" helps store and reuse knowledge.

#### From Edge to Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

This session introduced Amazon CloudFront and the role of CDN in modern systems.

Benefits of CloudFront include:

* Reducing latency when users access applications.
* Accelerating global content delivery.
* Strengthening security with AWS Shield and WAF.
* Optimizing operating costs.

Through this session, I understood more clearly how CloudFront works and the role of CDN in distributed systems.

#### Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Q

The speaker introduced Amazon Q, an enterprise AI assistant on the AWS platform.

Key capabilities include:

* Querying enterprise data using natural language.
* Supporting programming and document analysis.
* Automating workflows.
* Increasing employee productivity.

#### 36 Hours with LotusHacks: Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

Team VIB shared their journey of building a product during a 36-hour hackathon.

The development process included:

* Identifying a real-world problem.
* Designing a solution.
* Building a prototype.
* Completing and presenting the product.

This sharing session helped me better understand teamwork and product development under time pressure.

#### Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings

This session explained how large language models (LLMs) work.

Important concepts included:

* Tokens and token generation probabilities.
* Temperature, Top-K, and Top-P.
* Different results may appear even when using the same prompt.
* Techniques for reducing model variance.

As a result, I better understood how AI generates content and why hallucination can occur.

#### Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System

The speaker introduced the Multi-Agent AI model in enterprises.

Key topics included:

* Multiple AI agents collaborating to solve complex problems.
* Security and permission management.
* Building scalable AI systems.
* Applying the model to financial and enterprise problems.
